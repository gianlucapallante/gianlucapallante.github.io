Last week we wrapped up three days at Scuola Superiore Sant'Anna in Pisa on **"Macroeconomic Implications of Climate-Related Risks: Challenges and Opportunities for the Low-Carbon Transition"** 🌍

The workshop brought together researchers working on the macroeconomics of climate change, with a shared interest in understanding how physical and transition risks reshape economic dynamics, and what that means for the green transition.

Highlights included a keynote by **Jean-Francois Mercure** (University of Exeter), a roundtable with **Francesca Diluiso** (Bank of England), **Etienne Espagne** (Agence française de développement), and **Andrea Roventini** (Sant'Anna), as well as a dedicated Young Scholar Initiative session.

A few themes kept coming up across sessions: methodological diversity as both a strength and a coordination challenge, the gap between modelling insights and policy uptake, and what a shared language across approaches might actually look like in practice.

Another important takeaway was the value of building and sustaining a research community able to continue producing robust evidence, especially at a time when the winds are not always favourable for climate action. In moments like these, rigorous analysis, open dialogue, and intellectual collaboration matter even more.

A heartfelt thank-you to the young scholars, senior scholars, and discussants for the quality of their work, and to the organizers who made it all run smoothly!

A joint initiative by **Scuola Superiore Sant'Anna**, **IUSS - Scuola Universitaria Superiore Pavia**, and **Alma Mater Studiorum - Università di Bologna**, supported by the PRIN projects **ECliPTIc** and **NEWS** 📍
